## Part A — Positioning

### What we're actually selling

A **portrait-first mobile Lorcana play client, simulator, and creator toolkit** — built on a deterministic headless engine that powers Monte Carlo deck analytics, what-if branching replay, and clone-trainer bot infrastructure.

One-liner (from `docs/STRATEGY.md`): *"Lorcana, actually built for your phone. Portrait-first, one-handed, no rotation — plus a bot that doesn't suck, sim-based deck analytics, and real tools for creators."*

Three audience segments, three pitches, one unified claim ("Lorcana done right on mobile"):

1. **duels.ink users on mobile** — we're the better phone experience. Portrait-first is our signature.
2. **Inktable users (solo practice)** — we're the solo sim with a bot that actually works, plus real analytics.
3. **Creators** — we're the only toolkit shipping sandbox + branching replay + clip export + scenario URLs.

The brand has to carry all three without over-indexing on any one.

### Audience segments (ordered by acquisition priority)

| Segment | Today they use | Our wedge | Brand must signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **duels.ink users on phone** | duels.ink, landscape-on-phone | Portrait-first mobile UX. One-handed, no rotation. | "This feels right on my phone" — on first open, before features register |
| **Creators** (YT/Twitch/TikTok) | OBS + duels.ink screen recordings + handwritten annotations | Sandbox + branching replay + clip export | "This is a production tool, not just a play app" |
| **Inktable users / solo practice** | Inktable.net (bad bot, no MP) | Good bot. Real analytics. | "The sim Inktable should have been" |
| **Analytical players** (mid-to-top ladder) | Spreadsheets, vibes, Discord chat | Monte Carlo deck analysis + what-if replay | "Rigorous, deliberate, examines the game" |

Primary brand audience: **duels.ink users on phone first, creators second.** Every chrome/identity decision gets evaluated against "does this pull a duels.ink mobile user into trying us?" The other audiences are pitch-compatible and downstream.

### Tone register

Picked: **utilitarian-confident with a Lorcana-world hook.**

- **Utilitarian-confident** — short, punchy, no pretension. Figma, Linear, Arc, Cursor. Projects "this is a focused tool." Cuts against the fan-site amateurish default in the Lorcana third-party space.
- **Lorcana-world hook** — reference ink / lore / song / story / illumineer obliquely. Legibly on-theme to the niche without triggering IP risk.

Rejected registers:
- **Cute / portmanteau** ("Lorcanalytics") — reads fan-site.
- **Derivative / combat-y** ("DuelPro", "clashes.ink") — reads duels.ink clone.
- **Engine / backend-first** ("LorcanaDB", "InkEngine") — undersells the consumer-facing wedge.
- **Theatrical Disney pastiche** (display serifs, glittery accents) — risks appearing to cosplay as official.

### What the brand refuses to be

- **Not "Lorcana-branded."** No "Lorcana" in the name. Ravensburger tolerance has limits; App Store listing needs a defensible mark; future engine licensing needs a name that isn't riding the trademark.
- **Not a duels.ink clone.** No `.ink` domain punning on combat words. No overlapping color palette. No identical chrome.
- **Not "another Ink- tool."** The `Ink-` prefix namespace is saturated (Inktable, Inkdecks, plus `.ink` TLD convention). A name starting with "Ink" reads as a me-too entry in that category regardless of content.
- **Not pretending to be official.** No Disney-style typography, no Lorcana wordmark adjacency, no "powered by Disney" implication.
- **Not single-product.** Avoid names that only mean "video editor" ("Cliplab") or only mean "play client" ("Battlegrounds"). We'll have all three products under one roof.

### Visual/motion direction (for when a name lands)

These are downstream of naming but worth listing so candidates get evaluated against a coherent visual future:

- **Ink reference should be oblique.** duels.ink owns the straightforward "ink drop" visual. We reference ink through *flow / diffusion* (spreading in water), through *contrast* (white ink on dark), or through *illumination* (light-through-ink, manuscript gilding) — never through the basic drop silhouette.
- **Typography: modern sans with warmth.** Inter, General Sans, Söhne. Not Cormorant. Not Disney display serifs. We're a tool, not a storybook.
- **Color: neutral primary + unexpected accent.** The six Lorcana inks are taken visually (amber gold, amethyst violet, emerald green, ruby red, sapphire blue, steel gray). Using any one allies the brand with that color's decks. Better: near-black or deep navy primary, signature accent in a color *outside* the six — teal, warm bone, or a desaturated indigo reads "meta-level" instead of "I'm the Amber app."
- **Motion signature: deliberate, not flashy.** Ink diffusing, pages turning, spotlight sweeping. Every motion choice reads "this is for thinking about the game" not "this is for dopamine hits."

### IP risk rubric (applied to every candidate)

1. Must **not** contain "Lorcana."
2. Must **not** contain registered Ravensburger terms. **Illumineer** is almost certainly a Ravensburger mark — avoid in the product name. Acceptable as a descriptor in copy.
3. Must **not** use Disney character names or iconic Disney phrases.
4. Safe territory: English vocabulary derived from storytelling, printing, music, manuscripts, and light. These are genre-adjacent without being brand-adjacent.

### Domain strategy

Tiered preference:

1. **`.com`** — adult brand signal, required if we ever take payment or do a serious launch. Hard to get without compromise.
2. **`.studio` / `.app`** — creator-first, tool-first. Readable to the audience we care about.
3. **`.gg`** — overused; reads Twitch-teen. Accept only if the name demands it.
4. **`.ink`** — **avoid**. duels.ink has codified this TLD as "Lorcana play client." A `.ink` domain gets us filed as a clone on first glance.

### Parent/sub-brand question (unresolved)

One brand or two?

- **Single brand** (company = product): simpler, matches current scale. e.g., `Inklab` is both the company and the product.
- **Parent + child**: company name carries the engine/moat, product name carries the consumer app. e.g., company `Inklab`, product `Inkline`. Matters if the engine gets licensed or we ship a separate native play app later.

**My lean: single brand for now.** Revisit only if a licensing conversation materializes. Premature parent-branding is a common startup mistake.

## Part B — Name candidates

Generated by bucket, then stress-tested. Availability notes are guesses — every finalist needs a real domain/trademark search.

### Generation (33 candidates, by thematic bucket)

**Bucket 1 — Ink/printing (indirect):**

**Bucket 2 — Story/narrative:**
Prologue, Epilogue, Reprise, Retellings, Afterword, Overture, Intermission, Canticle, Encore

**Bucket 3 — Lorcana-world vocab (non-trademark):**
Lorecast, Lorelight, Tropes, Archive, Tome, Codex

**Bucket 4 — Laboratory/tool:**
Inklab, Atelier, Rehearsal, Scene, Bench

**Bucket 5 — Abstract/neologism:**
Muse, Gleam, Opus, Corpus, Refrain

### Second-pass replacements (after competitive sweep)

Added after learning about Inktable, Inkdecks, and the Lorecast collision:

- **Inkline** — added then demoted (Ink- prefix saturation)
- **Gloss** — manuscript-annotation meaning; creator/teaching-coded
- **Pressroom**, **Broadsheet** — printing-shop metaphors

### Stress test — five-filter scoring

| Filter | Weight |
|---|---|
| 1. IP safety (no Lorcana/Ravensburger/Disney marks) | pass/fail (fail = disqualified) |
| 2. Clone/namespace risk (not duels.ink-shaped, not Ink- prefixed) | 0–3 |
| 3. Audience fit (duels.ink user + creator + solo + analyst) | 0–3 |
| 4. Scalability (covers all three pitches + future) | 0–3 |
| 5. Availability likelihood (.com or strong alt TLD) | 0–3 |

### Disqualified

- **Lorecast** — it's the name of the official Disney Lorcana weekly stream. Direct collision with Ravensburger's own content program; undefendable.
- **Illumineer** / **Illumineers** — Ravensburger mark for the player persona.
- **Dreamborn** / **Dreamlight** — Dreamborn.ink exists; Disney Dreamlight Valley collision.
- **Inkwell** — on-nose; likely fan-site use.
- **Opus** — Opus Clip, Anthropic Opus model, etc.
- **Codex** — GitHub/OpenAI Codex collisions.
- **Muse** — Notion/Figma adjacency.
- **Tropes** — TVTropes.org prior art; would be confused in conversation.
- **Inkline**, **Inklab** — Ink- prefix namespace saturated by Inktable and Inkdecks. Would read as me-too.

### Revised shortlist (post-competitive-sweep)

| Name | IP | Namespace | Audience | Scale | Avail | Total | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Reprise** | ✓ | 3 | 3 | 3 | 2 | **11** | Musical repeat → replay/branching tie. Disney-song-adjacent without touching Disney IP. Warm + serious. "Rehearse / examine / teach" is exactly what a reprise is. Best fit for the revised positioning. |
| **Gloss** | ✓ | 3 | 3 | 2 | 2 | **10** | Medieval manuscript annotation term. Short, tool-shaped, creator-coded. Strong on the annotation/teaching surface. Narrower semantic — leans creator, harder to stretch to mobile play client. |
| **Refrain** | ✓ | 3 | 2 | 2 | 2 | **9** | Repeated part of a song → replay/branching. Same bucket as Reprise but with a negative secondary meaning ("refrain from") the brand has to overcome. |
| **Folio** | ✓ | 3 | 2 | 3 | 2 | **10** | Old printing format. Good scalability. Softer signal — "collection" more than "practice." |
| **Prologue** | ✓ | 3 | 3 | 2 | 1 | **9** | Elegant, scalable. `.com` certainly taken; needs creative TLD. |

### Recommendation

**Reprise** is now my lead pick. The revised strategy is explicitly about giving users a place to *rehearse* — portrait-first practice, what-if replay branching, creator-produced teaching content. A reprise is literally a deliberate re-performance for mastery. The word does exactly what the product does. It also survives every filter: IP-clean, outside the Ink- namespace, warm enough for the duels.ink-user migration pitch, serious enough for the creator and analyst pitches, Disney-song-adjacent without trademark touch.

Fallback: **Gloss** if Reprise is unavailable in acceptable TLDs. Narrower but distinctive; strong on the creator-annotation surface.

### Next steps

1. **Domain + trademark sweep** on Reprise, Gloss, Folio, Refrain, Prologue. USPTO + EUIPO live marks. `.com`, `.app`, `.studio`, `.gg`. Do NOT pursue `.ink`.
2. **Lorcana-community sweep** (the check I missed for Lorecast): Google "[name] lorcana", search major Lorcana podcasts / streams / tool names, check Discord vanity handles. This is the step that caught Lorecast; it should run for every finalist.
3. **Say-it-out-loud test** with a Lorcana-player friend: "I'm making a mobile Lorcana app called [X]" — what do they guess it does? Gap between guess and intent = brand-gap.
4. **Logo/wordmark sketch** on the survivor. Tone: modern sans + warmth, ink diffusion (not drops), neutral + unexpected accent.
5. **Temporary placeholder swap** — leave `<title>Lorcana Sim</title>` in place until naming lands. Changing twice is worse than changing once.

## Open questions

- **Scope ambition.** Lorcana-only brand forever, or multi-TCG upside? Affects how Lorcana-coded the name should be. Reprise/Gloss/Folio generalize well beyond Lorcana; their semantics are TCG-agnostic. Would survive a future pivot.
- **Voice register on the marketing surface.** "Serious tool" (Linear/Figma tone) vs. "warm and community-y" (Notion/Arc tone). Given the revised positioning leads with mobile-UX against duels.ink, the voice probably needs to be *warmer than Linear, more confident than Notion* — closer to Arc or Things. Revisit during wordmark work.
- **Defensive domain bundle.** Worth reserving Reprise + Gloss + Folio in our preferred TLDs for ~$50 total while deciding. Cheap optionality that prevents a "the name we wanted got registered last Tuesday" failure mode.
